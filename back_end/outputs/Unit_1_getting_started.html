Unit 1: Leisure Activities

Lesson: Getting Started

I. OBJECTIVES

  • To introduce students to vocabulary related to leisure activities.
  • To practice listening and reading comprehension skills.
  • To use the present continuous and simple present tense in context.

II. MATERIALS

  • Audio recording for 'Listen and read' task
  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard
  • Markers

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To engage students and introduce the topic of leisure activities.
  • Content: Brainstorming session about leisure activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Greet the class and ask students about their favorite leisure activities. Write some of their answers on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's questions and share their favorite leisure activities.
Stage 1: Listen and Read (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice listening and reading skills using the dialogue.
  • Content: Trang is looking for a knitting kit. Tom loves spending free time...
  • Target Language/Details: knitting kit, spending free time, is looking, loves
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio recording of the dialogue. Ask students to listen and follow along in their textbooks.
Students Listen to the audio and read along in their textbooks.
Teacher After listening, ask students to underline the words 'knitting kit' and 'spending free time' in their textbooks.
Stage 2: Read and Complete (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To check comprehension and reinforce vocabulary and grammar.
  • Content: Complete the sentences using the dialogue.
  • Target Language/Details: is looking, loves, spends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to read the conversation again and complete the sentences in their textbooks.
Students Read the conversation again and fill in the blanks with the correct words.
Teacher Check answers as a class and provide feedback.
Stage 3: Pair Work Activities (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice writing and speaking skills using the target vocabulary and grammar.
  • Content: Write the activities you and your partner enjoy doing in your free time.
  • Target Language/Details: activities, is looking, loves, spends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Instruct students to work in pairs and write down activities they enjoy doing in their free time using the present continuous and simple present tense.
Students Work in pairs to discuss and write down their leisure activities.
Teacher Ask some pairs to share their activities with the class.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Content: Review of vocabulary and grammar points covered in the lesson.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, present continuous tense, simple present tense
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Summarize the key points of the lesson and ask students what they learned today.
Students Reflect on the lesson and share what they have learned.
