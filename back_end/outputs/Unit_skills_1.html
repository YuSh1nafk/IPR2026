Leisure Time

Lesson: SKILLS 1

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Students will be able to identify and discuss various leisure activities.
  • Students will improve their reading comprehension skills by answering questions about a text.
  • Students will practice speaking skills by discussing and reporting on leisure activities.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Pictures of leisure activities
  • Board and markers
  • Worksheets with reading comprehension questions
  • Table for recording group answers

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of leisure activities and engage students.
  • Content: The teacher will introduce the topic by discussing common leisure activities and showing pictures.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Show pictures of different leisure activities and ask students to name them.
Students Look at the pictures and identify the activities they see.
Stage 1: Look at the Pictures (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To activate students' prior knowledge about leisure activities.
  • Content: Students will look at pictures and identify the activities they can see.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to look at the pictures in the textbook and discuss what activities they can see.
Students Discuss in pairs or small groups the activities they see in the pictures.
Teacher Invite a few students to share their observations with the class.
Stage 2: Reading Comprehension - Multiple Choice (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice reading comprehension by choosing the correct answers.
  • Content: Students will read about Trang's leisure activities and choose the correct answers to questions.
  • Target Language/Details: Connect, Family, Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide students with the text about Trang's leisure activities and the multiple-choice questions.
Students Read the text and select the correct answer for each question.
Teacher Review the answers with the class and discuss the correct options.
Stage 3: Reading Comprehension - Answer the Questions (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To enhance understanding of the text by answering detailed questions.
  • Content: Students will read the text again and answer specific questions about Trang's leisure activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Cook, Recipes, Family members
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to read the text again and answer the questions provided.
Students Individually answer the questions based on the text.
Teacher Discuss the answers with the class and provide feedback.
Stage 4: Group Discussion - Leisure Activities (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To encourage speaking practice by discussing personal leisure activities.
  • Content: Students will discuss their own leisure activities in groups and record their answers.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, Family members
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Divide students into groups and provide them with questions to discuss.
Students Take turns asking and answering questions about their leisure activities and feelings.
Teacher Ask students to record their group members' answers in a table.
Stage 5: Reporting - Common Leisure Activities (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice reporting skills by sharing group findings with the class.
  • Content: Students will report their group's answers to the class and identify common activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, Common activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Invite each group to report their findings to the class.
Students Present their group's answers and discuss which activities are most common.
Teacher Summarize the common leisure activities mentioned by the groups.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and reinforce key vocabulary and concepts.
  • Content: The teacher will review the key points from the lesson and ask students to reflect on what they learned.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, Connect, Family members
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to share one new thing they learned about leisure activities or family time.
Students Reflect on the lesson and share their thoughts with the class.
