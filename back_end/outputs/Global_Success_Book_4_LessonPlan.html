FULL LESSON PLAN

Lesson: Getting Started

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Understand and use vocabulary related to leisure activities, such as DIY, knitting kit, leisure time, hang out, cinema, comedy.
  • Practice the present continuous and simple present tenses in the context of talking about hobbies and leisure activities.
  • Develop listening and reading comprehension skills through dialogues.
  • Engage in pair and group activities to practice speaking and writing skills.

II. MATERIALS

  • Audio recording of the dialogue
  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed copies of the dialogue for students
  • Pictures for activity matching

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of leisure activities and engage students' interest.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students: 'What do you like to do in your free time?' and write their responses on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's question with their favorite leisure activities.
Stage 1: Listen and Read (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the dialogue and practice listening and reading skills.
  • Target Language/Details: I’m keen on doing DIY, knitting kit, leisure time, hang out, cinema, comedy.
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio recording of the dialogue.
Students Listen to the recording and follow the text in their books.
Teacher Ask students to read the dialogue aloud in pairs.
Stage 2: Complete the Sentences (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To check comprehension of the dialogue and practice sentence completion.
  • Target Language/Details: knitting kit, leisure time, free time, together, Sunday
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Instruct students to complete the sentences on page 8.
Students Complete the sentences: 1. Trang is looking for a _________. 2. She likes doing DIY in her _________. 3. Tom loves spending his free time with _________. 4. Tom and his friends usually _________ together. 5. Tom, Mark, Trang, and Mai are going to see a comedy this _________.
Stage 3: Match Activities with Pictures (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice vocabulary related to leisure activities by matching words to pictures.
  • Target Language/Details: doing DIY, doing puzzles, surfing the net, cooking, playing sport, messaging friends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Display the images on the board and ask students to match the activities from the box with the pictures.
Students Work in pairs to write the activities under the correct pictures.
Stage 4: Guess the Activities (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice understanding descriptions and matching them to activities.
  • Target Language/Details: improve memory, save money, improve physical health, keep in touch, learn something about IT
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Read the phrases aloud and ask students to guess the activities.
Students Work in pairs to match the phrases with the activities from Stage 3.
Stage 5: Group Discussion (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice speaking by discussing weekend plans and reporting to the class.
  • Target Language/Details: If you have some free time this weekend, what will you do?
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to discuss the question: 'If you have some free time this weekend, what will you do?' in groups.
Students Discuss in groups and then report their answers to the class.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities vocabulary
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Review the key vocabulary and grammar points from the lesson.
Students Participate in the review by answering questions.

Lesson: A Closer Look 1

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Understand and use expressions related to likes and dislikes such as 'be fond of', 'be keen on', 'be interested in', 'be crazy about', 'be into'.
  • Practice using negation in expressions of likes and dislikes by adding 'not' after the verb 'be'.
  • Improve pronunciation skills by distinguishing between the sounds /ʊ/ and /u:/.

II. MATERIALS

  • Audio recording for pronunciation practice
  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed vocabulary matching exercise
  • Printed fill-in-the-blank sentences

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of likes and dislikes and engage students' interest.
  • Target Language/Details: Likes and dislikes expressions
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students: 'What are some activities you like or dislike?' and write their responses on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's question with activities they like or dislike.
Stage 1: Vocabulary Matching (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To match words and phrases to form expressions about likes.
  • Target Language/Details: be fond of, be keen on, be interested in, be crazy about, be into
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Explain the task and demonstrate one example. Then ask students to complete the matching exercise.
Students Work individually to match the words and phrases in column A with those in column B.
Stage 2: Negation of Likes and Dislikes (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice changing positive expressions about likes into negative ones.
  • Target Language/Details: Add 'not' after 'be' to express dislikes
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Explain how to change expressions of likes to dislikes by adding 'not' after 'be'. Provide examples.
Students Practice changing given sentences from positive to negative.
Stage 3: Fill in the Blanks (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice using vocabulary in context by filling in blanks with correct words.
  • Target Language/Details: fond, interested, keen, into, about
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Instruct students to fill in the blanks with the correct words.
Students Complete the sentences: 1. I'm ________ of taking photos. 2. Mai is ________ in history. 3. My sister is ________ on cooking. 4. My brother spends lots of time surfing the net, but I'm not ________ it. 5. I'm not crazy ________ doing DIY.
Stage 4: Sentence Completion (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To express personal likes and dislikes using given vocabulary.
  • Target Language/Details: crazy about, keen on, fond of, not interested in, not into
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Ask students to complete the sentences with words from the box or their own ideas.
Students Write sentences: 1. I'm crazy about ________. 2. I'm keen on ________. 3. I'm fond of ________. 4. I'm not interested in ________. 5. I'm not into ________.
Stage 5: Pronunciation Practice (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice pronunciation of /ʊ/ and /u:/ sounds.
  • Target Language/Details: Words with /ʊ/ and /u:/ sounds
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Play the audio recording and ask students to repeat the words.
Students Listen and repeat: cook, school, group, would, push, woman, June, move. Then categorize the words into /ʊ/ and /u:/ columns.
Stage 6: Sentence Pronunciation Practice (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice pronunciation in context by identifying /ʊ/ and /u:/ sounds in sentences.
  • Target Language/Details: Underline /ʊ/ words, circle /u:/ words
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio of sentences and instruct students to underline /ʊ/ words and circle /u:/ words.
Students Listen and practice: 1. She likes reading books and swimming in the pool. 2. When it is cool, we like to play football. 3. She drew and made puddings in her free time. 4. My mum loves pumpkin soup and coffee with a little sugar. 5. My brother is fond of watching the cartoon about a clever wolf.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Target Language/Details: Expressions of likes and dislikes, /ʊ/ and /u:/ sounds
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Summarize the lesson's key vocabulary and pronunciation points.
Students Participate in the review by asking questions or sharing what they learned.

Lesson: A Closer Look 2

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Understand and use verbs of liking and disliking followed by gerunds and to-infinitives.
  • Practice distinguishing between verbs that can only be followed by gerunds and those that can be followed by both gerunds and to-infinitives.
  • Improve sentence completion skills using verbs of liking and disliking.
  • Engage in interactive activities to reinforce the use of likes and dislikes vocabulary.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed verb lists for categorization
  • Audio recording for pronunciation practice
  • Printed fill-in-the-blank sentences

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of verbs of liking and disliking and engage students' interest.
  • Target Language/Details: Verbs of liking and disliking
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students: 'What activities do you love or hate doing?' and write their responses on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's question with activities they love or hate.
Stage 1: Verb Categorization (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To categorize verbs based on whether they are followed by gerunds only or both gerunds and to-infinitives.
  • Target Language/Details: love, dislike, like, enjoy, detest, hate, fancy, prefer
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Explain the difference between verbs followed by gerunds only and those followed by both gerunds and to-infinitives. Provide examples.
Students Work in pairs to categorize the verbs into the correct columns.
Stage 2: Multiple Choice Exercise (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice choosing the correct form of verbs in sentences.
  • Target Language/Details: travelling, doing, to watch, going, hunting
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the task and read the sentences aloud.
Students Individually choose the correct answer A, B, or C for each sentence: 1. I love ________, so in my leisure time, I go to some villages near Ha Noi to relax and enjoy the outdoors. 2. Tom enjoys ________ puzzles, especially Sudoku. 3. When do you like ________ TV? 4. Do you fancy ________ to the cinema this weekend? 5. I detest ________. I think it's cruel to harm animals.
Stage 3: Sentence Completion with Pictures (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To complete sentences using verbs in brackets based on pictures.
  • Target Language/Details: like, enjoy, dislike, hate, prefer
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Show the pictures and instruct students to complete the sentences using the verbs in brackets.
Students Write the sentences: 1. Mark __________ (like) 2. The girls __________ (enjoy) 3. My cousin __________ (dislike) 4. My father __________ (hate) 5. Tom and his sister __________ (prefer)
Stage 4: Personal Sentence Completion (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To express personal likes and dislikes using the target vocabulary.
  • Target Language/Details: I like, I prefer, I love, I dislike, I hate, I detest
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Ask students to complete the sentences about themselves.
Students Write sentences: I like ______. I prefer ______. I love ______. I dislike ______. I hate ______. I detest ______.
Stage 5: Game - Likes and Dislikes Mimes (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice asking and answering questions about likes and dislikes through a mime game.
  • Target Language/Details: Do you like, Do you love, Yes, I do, No, I don't
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the rules of the mime game and provide an example.
Students Work in groups. One student mimes a leisure activity they like or dislike. The others guess by asking yes/no questions using the verbs they have learned. Example: A: Do you like surfing the net? B: No, I don't. C: Do you love messaging your friends? B: Yes, I do.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Target Language/Details: Verbs of liking and disliking, gerunds, to-infinitives
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Summarize the lesson's key grammar points and vocabulary.
Students Participate in the review by asking questions or sharing what they learned.

Lesson: Communication

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Understand and use phrases for inviting and accepting invitations.
  • Practice speaking skills through role-play and pair work.
  • Identify leisure activities from pictures and descriptions.
  • Discuss and report on personal preferences regarding leisure activities.

II. MATERIALS

  • Audio recording of dialogues
  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed dialogue scripts
  • Printed table for information completion

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of inviting and accepting invitations and engage students' interest.
  • Target Language/Details: Inviting and accepting invitations
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students: 'Have you invited anyone to do something recently? How did you invite them?' and write key phrases on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's question with examples of invitations they have given or received.
Stage 1: Listen and Read Dialogues (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To listen and understand dialogues that include invitations and responses.
  • Target Language/Details: Would you like to..., I’d love to, Do you fancy..., That’s great
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Play the audio recording of the dialogues and ask students to follow along in their textbooks.
Students Listen to the dialogues and pay attention to the highlighted parts.
Teacher Ask students to read the dialogues aloud in pairs, focusing on pronunciation and intonation.
Stage 2: Practise Inviting and Accepting (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice inviting and accepting invitations using the target language.
  • Target Language/Details: Would you like to..., I’d love to, Do you fancy..., That’s great
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the task and provide examples of how to invite and accept invitations.
Students Work in pairs to practice the dialogues in the given situations: - Invite your friend to play badminton. - Invite your friend to make paper flowers with you. - Invite your friend to try your home-made pizza.
Stage 3: Identify Activities in Pictures (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To identify leisure activities from pictures and discuss them.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Show the pictures and ask students to discuss what activity is depicted in each picture.
Students Work in pairs to answer the question: 'What is the activity in each picture?' and share their answers with the class.
Stage 4: Complete the Table (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To read descriptions of leisure activities and complete a table with information.
  • Target Language/Details: Origami, Snowboarding, Playing sport, Relax, Health, Strength
Role Activities (Step-by-step)




Teacher Ask students to read the text about the three teenagers and complete the table with the information provided.
Students Fill in the table with details about each teenager's leisure activity, the country they are from, who they do the activity with, and the benefits of the activity.
Stage 5: Group Discussion (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To discuss personal preferences for leisure activities and report to the class.
  • Target Language/Details: Which activity do you want to try?, Why do you want to try it?
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the discussion task and encourage students to think about their own preferences.
Students Work in groups to discuss the questions: 'Which of the activities in 4 do you want to try?' and 'Why do you want to try it?' Then prepare to report their group's answers to the class.
Students Report their group's answers to the class, explaining their reasons for choosing the activities.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Target Language/Details: Inviting and accepting invitations, leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Summarize the lesson's key points and ask students if they have any questions.
Students Participate in the review by asking questions or sharing what they learned.

Lesson: Skills 1

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Develop reading comprehension skills by identifying key information in a text about leisure activities.
  • Enhance speaking skills through group discussions and reporting.
  • Practice using the present simple tense to describe habitual actions.
  • Improve question formation using 'What,' 'Who,' and 'Which'.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed copies of the text about Trang's leisure activities
  • Printed question sheets for group discussions

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of leisure activities and engage students' interest.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students: 'What are your favorite leisure activities?' and write their responses on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's question with their favorite leisure activities.
Stage 1: Picture Discussion (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To identify and discuss leisure activities depicted in pictures.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Show pictures of various leisure activities and ask students to identify them.
Students Discuss in pairs: 'What activities can you see in the pictures?' and share their answers with the class.
Stage 2: Reading Comprehension - Multiple Choice (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To read about Trang's leisure activities and choose the correct answers to comprehension questions.
  • Target Language/Details: Connect, recipes, family members
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Distribute the text about Trang's leisure activities and explain the multiple-choice questions.
Students Read the text and choose the correct answer for each question: 1. The text is about Trang's leisure activities ______. A. in the past B. with her friends C. with her family 2. The word 'connect' is closest in meaning to ______. A. join with something B. better understand someone C. speak to someone by phone.
Stage 3: Reading Comprehension - Answering Questions (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To read the text again and answer detailed comprehension questions.
  • Target Language/Details: Present simple tense, question formation
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to read the text again and answer the following questions.
Students Write answers to the questions: 1. What does Trang do with her family members at the weekend? 2. Who looks for recipes when Trang and her brother cook? 3. Which leisure activity does she love the most? 4. What does Trang's mum teach her to do? 5. What did she win?
Stage 4: Group Discussion and Recording (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice speaking skills by discussing personal leisure activities and recording peers' answers.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, feelings
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide question sheets and explain the group discussion task.
Students Discuss in groups: 1. What leisure activities do you usually do with your family? 2. Which one do you like the most? Why? 3. How do you feel when you spend time with your family members? Record your friends' answers in the table.


Stage 5: Reporting Group Findings (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To report on group discussions and identify common leisure activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Report, common activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask each group to report their findings to the class.
Students Present their group's answers and discuss which activities are the most common among their peers.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, present simple tense
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Summarize the lesson's key vocabulary and grammar points.
Students Participate in the review by asking questions or sharing what they learned.

Lesson: Looking Back

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Review and consolidate vocabulary related to leisure activities.
  • Practice forming complete sentences from given cues using appropriate verb forms.
  • Enhance understanding of gerunds following verbs of liking and disliking.
  • Improve grammar skills by filling in blanks with correct verb forms.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed copies of sentence cues
  • Printed verb form exercises
  • Printed passage completion exercise

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of leisure activities and engage students' interest.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students: 'What are some leisure activities you enjoy?' and write their responses on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's question with their favorite leisure activities.
Stage 1: Vocabulary Completion (10 minutes)

  • Target Language/Details: Online, puzzles, DIY, messaging, fond of, keen on
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the task and provide examples of leisure activities.
Students Complete the sentences: 1. Mai loves __________ online for about 30 minutes a day. She thinks puzzles are good for the brain. 2. My favourite leisure activity is __________. I can make many things myself, such as paper flowers and bracelets. 3. __________ is a popular way for teens to spend their free time. Many of them send messages to each other every day. 4. My brother is fond of __________ with his friends. On Sundays, he usually plays football, goes swimming, or plays badminton with them. 5. Tom spends one hour on the Internet almost every day. He is keen on __________.
Stage 2: Sentence Construction (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To write complete sentences from given cues using correct verb forms.
  • Target Language/Details: Crazy about, interested, not fond, not into, keen on
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide sentence cues and explain how to form complete sentences.
Students Write sentences: 1. my cousin / crazy about / play / computer / games. 2. they / interested / play / badminton / after school? 3. I / not fond / make models / because / I / not patient. 4. - why / you / not into / cook? - because / often / burn myself. 5. my friends / keen / do judo / and / they / go / judo club / every Sunday.
Stage 3: Grammar Practice - Gerunds (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To fill in blanks with the correct form of gerunds following verbs of liking and disliking.
  • Target Language/Details: Enjoy cycling, don’t like reading, detests playing, love chatting, prefer doing
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the use of gerunds after verbs of liking and disliking.
Students Complete the sentences: 1. Does Tom enjoy (cycle) __________ in the park with his friends? 2. Some teenagers don’t like (read) __________ comic books. 3. Mai detests (play) __________ sport because it’s tiring. 4. Trang and Ann love (chat) __________ with each other in their free time. 5. What do Nam and Mark prefer (do) __________ at the weekend?
Stage 4: Passage Completion (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To complete a passage using the correct verb forms and additional words if necessary.
  • Target Language/Details: Riding, reading, messaging, making, knitting, playing
Role Activities (Step-by-step)


Teacher Provide the passage and explain how to use the verbs in brackets to complete it.
Students Complete the passage: Ann is my best friend. She usually has free time at the weekend. She enjoys (1. ride) riding a horse at the riding club. Sometimes, she likes (2. read) ______ or (3. message) ______. She also loves (4. make) ______ and (5. knit) ______. However, there is one thing she doesn’t like doing in her free time. She dislikes (6. play) ______.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the lesson and consolidate learning.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, gerunds, sentence construction
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Summarize the lesson's key vocabulary and grammar points.
Students Participate in the review by asking questions or sharing what they learned.

Lesson: Project

  • Report findings using structured responses based on collected data.
  • Use vocabulary related to leisure activities and expressions of likes and dislikes.

  • Textbook

  • Printed copies of the interview questions

  • Audio recording for pronunciation practice

Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Distribute printed copies of the interview questions and explain the task.

What leisure activities would they like to try in the future?

Role Activities (Step-by-step)

Students Listen and repeat the words and sentences, focusing on the correct pronunciation of /uː/ and /ʊ/ sounds.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
