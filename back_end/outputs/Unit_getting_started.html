Leisure Activities

Lesson: GETTING STARTED

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Students will be able to understand and use vocabulary related to DIY and leisure activities.
  • Students will practice reading and listening skills through dialogues.
  • Students will be able to express their preferences and future intentions in English.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Audio player
  • Board and markers
  • Worksheets for sentence completion
  • Pictures of DIY activities

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To engage students and introduce the topic of leisure activities.
  • Content: The teacher will introduce the topic of DIY and leisure activities by discussing common hobbies.
  • Target Language/Details: DIY, leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Greet the students and ask them about their hobbies. Write some examples on the board.
Students Respond to the teacher's questions about their hobbies.
Stage 1: Listen and Read (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the dialogue and practice listening and reading skills.
  • Content: Students will listen to the dialogue between Tom and Trang and then read along.
  • Target Language/Details: DIY, knitting kit, leisure time, dollhouses, paper flowers, hang out, cinema, comedy
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio of the dialogue while students listen.
Students Listen to the audio and follow along in their textbooks.
Teacher Ask students to read the dialogue aloud in pairs.
Students Read the dialogue in pairs, taking turns to play Tom and Trang.
Stage 2: Complete the Sentences (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To check comprehension of the dialogue through sentence completion.
  • Content: Students will complete sentences based on the dialogue.
  • Target Language/Details: knitting kit, leisure time, free time, cinema, Sunday
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Distribute worksheets with incomplete sentences from the dialogue.
Students Complete the sentences individually using information from the dialogue.
Teacher Review the answers with the class.
Stage 3: Work in Pairs (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice making connections between phrases and activities.
  • Content: Students will match phrases with activities described in the dialogue.
  • Target Language/Details: improve memory, save money, improve physical health, keep in touch, learn something about IT
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Explain the phrases and ask students to match them with activities in pairs.
Students Work in pairs to match phrases with the correct activities.
Teacher Check the answers with the class.
Stage 4: Work in Groups (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To encourage speaking practice by discussing weekend plans.
  • Content: Students will discuss their weekend plans and report to the class.
  • Target Language/Details: free time, weekend plans
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to form groups and discuss what they will do if they have free time this weekend.
Students Discuss in groups and prepare to report their answers.
Teacher Ask each group to report their discussion to the class.
Students Report their group’s discussion to the class.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the key vocabulary and concepts learned in the lesson.
  • Content: The teacher will review the key vocabulary and ask students what they learned.
  • Target Language/Details: DIY, leisure activities, preferences
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to share one new word or phrase they learned today.
Students Share a new word or phrase they learned during the lesson.
