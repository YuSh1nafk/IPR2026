Leisure Time

Lesson: PROJECT

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Students will be able to conduct interviews using structured questions about leisure activities.
  • Students will collect and organize data from their interviews in a table.
  • Students will report and discuss the findings from their group interviews.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Interview question sheets
  • Table templates for data collection
  • Board and markers

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the concept of conducting surveys and interviews about leisure activities.
  • Content: The teacher will introduce the project by discussing the importance of understanding leisure activities among peers.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure time, Free time, Activities, Interview
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students what they think are common leisure activities among their peers.
Students Share their thoughts on common leisure activities.
Stage 1: Preparing for the Interview (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To prepare students for conducting interviews using structured questions.
  • Content: Students will review the interview questions and practice asking them.
  • Target Language/Details: When do you have free time?, What do you like doing with your family?, What leisure activities do you like doing on your own?
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide students with the list of interview questions and explain how to conduct an interview.
Students Pair up and practice asking and answering the interview questions.
Stage 2: Conducting the Interviews (20 minutes)
  • Aim: To conduct interviews with classmates to gather data about leisure activities.
  • Content: Students will interview their classmates using the prepared questions.
  • Target Language/Details: Family, Friends, Popular activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Divide students into groups and assign them to interview classmates from different groups.
Students Conduct interviews with classmates, recording their answers in the provided table template.
Stage 3: Collecting and Organizing Data (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To collect and organize the data gathered from interviews into a table.
  • Content: Students will organize the data from their interviews into a table format.
  • Target Language/Details: Collect answers, Use the table
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide students with a table template to organize their interview data.
Students Work in their groups to fill in the table with the collected data.
Stage 4: Reporting Findings (20 minutes)
  • Aim: To report and discuss the findings of the group’s interviews with the class.
  • Content: Students will report their findings to the class using guiding questions.
  • Target Language/Details: How many students did you interview?, Most popular activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask each group to use the guiding questions to prepare a short report of their findings.
Students Prepare and present their group’s findings to the class, highlighting the most popular activities.
Teacher Facilitate a class discussion on the findings and any interesting patterns observed.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the project and reflect on the findings from the interviews.
  • Content: The teacher will review the key points from the project and discuss what students learned about leisure activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities, Interview, Findings
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to reflect on what they learned about their classmates' leisure activities.
Students Share their reflections and discuss any surprising findings from the project.
