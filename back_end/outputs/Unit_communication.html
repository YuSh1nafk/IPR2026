Leisure Time

Lesson: COMMUNICATION

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Students will be able to use language for inviting and accepting invitations in various contexts.
  • Students will be able to practice and apply vocabulary related to leisure activities.
  • Students will develop speaking skills through pair and group work.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Audio player
  • Board and markers
  • Worksheets with dialogues
  • Table for leisure activities information

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of inviting and accepting invitations.
  • Content: The teacher will introduce the concept of inviting and accepting invitations by discussing common social activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Inviting, Accepting invitations
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students about recent invitations they have received or extended.
Students Share experiences of inviting or being invited to activities.
Stage 1: Listen and Read (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To familiarize students with the language of inviting and accepting invitations.
  • Content: Students will listen to and read dialogues, focusing on highlighted language for inviting and accepting invitations.
  • Target Language/Details: Would you like to...? Sure, I'd love to! How about...? That sounds great!
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio of the dialogues and ask students to follow along in their textbooks.
Students Listen to the dialogues and read along, paying attention to the highlighted parts.
Teacher Discuss the highlighted language and its use in invitations.
Stage 2: Pair Practice - Inviting and Accepting (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice using inviting and accepting language in conversations.
  • Content: Students will practice inviting and accepting invitations in specific scenarios using the language from Stage 1.
  • Target Language/Details: Play badminton, Make paper flowers, Home-made pizza
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide students with scenarios for practice and remind them to use the highlighted language.
Students Work in pairs to practice inviting and accepting invitations for playing badminton, making paper flowers, and trying home-made pizza.
Teacher Monitor the pairs and provide feedback on their use of language.
Stage 3: Pair Work - Answer the Question (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To encourage students to discuss leisure activities.
  • Content: Students will work in pairs to answer a question related to leisure activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to discuss a question related to leisure activities in pairs.
Students Discuss the question with their partner and share their thoughts.
Teacher Invite a few pairs to share their discussions with the class.
Stage 4: Reading and Completing the Table (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To gather information about leisure activities from a text and organize it in a table.
  • Content: Students will read what three teenagers say about their leisure activities and complete a table with the information.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide students with the text and a table to fill in with information about the teenagers' leisure activities.
Students Read the text and complete the table with the relevant information.
Teacher Review the completed tables with the class and discuss any discrepancies.
Stage 5: Group Discussion - Leisure Activities (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice speaking skills by discussing and reporting on leisure activities.
  • Content: Students will discuss which leisure activities they want to try and report their group's answers to the class.
  • Target Language/Details: Which activity do you want to try? Why do you want to try it?
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Divide students into groups and provide questions for discussion.
Students Discuss the questions in their groups and decide on their answers.
Teacher Ask each group to report their answers to the class.
Students Report their group's discussion to the class.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the key language and activities from the lesson.
  • Content: The teacher will review the key language and activities discussed in the lesson.
  • Target Language/Details: Inviting, Accepting invitations, Leisure activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to share one new phrase or activity they learned today.
Students Share what they learned about inviting, accepting invitations, or leisure activities.
