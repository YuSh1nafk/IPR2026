Unit 1: Leisure time

Lesson: Leisure Activities and Preferences

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Identify and use vocabulary related to leisure activities
  • Express likes and dislikes using appropriate verbs and structures
  • Differentiate and pronounce the sounds /u/ and /u:/ correctly
  • Read and comprehend a passage about leisure activities with family
  • Discuss leisure activities with family
  • Listen for specific information about leisure activities with friends
  • Write an email about leisure activities with friends
  • Practice inviting and accepting invitations in English

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Audio player
  • Flashcards with leisure activities
  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Handouts with reading passages
  • Sample email format
  • Pronunciation audio tracks

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To engage students and activate prior knowledge about leisure activities
  • Content: Students will discuss their favorite leisure activities.
Role Activities
Teacher Ask students about their favorite leisure activities and write some examples on the board.
Students Share their favorite leisure activities in pairs and then with the class.
Presentation (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce vocabulary and grammar related to leisure activities and preferences
  • Content: Introduce vocabulary related to leisure activities and expressions of likes and dislikes. Explain verbs of liking/disliking with gerunds and to-infinitives.
Role Activities
Teacher Present vocabulary using flashcards and model pronunciation.
Teacher Explain grammar rules for verbs of liking/disliking with examples.
Students Listen and repeat vocabulary and grammar examples.
Practice (20 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice using the new vocabulary and grammar structures in controlled activities
  • Content: Students will complete exercises using verbs of liking/disliking and practice pronunciation of /u/ and /u:/ sounds.
Role Activities
Teacher Distribute handouts with fill-in-the-blank and matching exercises.
Students Complete the exercises individually and then check answers with a partner.
Teacher Play audio tracks and have students practice pronunciation of /u/ and /u:/.
Production (25 minutes)
  • Aim: To provide opportunities for students to use the language in meaningful contexts
  • Content: Students will discuss leisure activities with a partner and write an email about leisure activities.
Role Activities
Students Discuss leisure activities they enjoy with their family in pairs.
Teacher Provide a sample email format and guide students in writing an email about leisure activities with friends.
Students Write and exchange emails with a partner for peer review.
Wrap-up (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To consolidate learning and practice everyday English expressions
  • Content: Review key vocabulary and grammar. Practice inviting and accepting invitations.
Role Activities
Teacher Review the lesson's key points and address any questions.
Students Role-play inviting and accepting invitations with a partner.
