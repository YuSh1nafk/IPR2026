Leisure Time

Lesson: SKILLS 2

I. OBJECTIVES

  • Students will be able to discuss and express opinions on leisure activities with friends.
  • Students will improve listening comprehension skills by identifying specific information from an interview.
  • Students will practice writing skills by composing an email about their leisure activities.

II. MATERIALS

  • Textbook
  • Audio player
  • Board and markers
  • Worksheets with listening comprehension questions
  • Paper for email writing

III. PROCEDURES

Warm-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To introduce the topic of leisure activities with friends and engage students.
  • Content: The teacher will introduce the topic by discussing the importance of spending leisure time with friends.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure time, Free time, Friends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students why they think spending time with friends is important.
Students Share their opinions on the importance of spending time with friends.
Stage 1: Answer the Questions (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To activate students' prior knowledge about leisure activities with friends.
  • Content: Students will answer questions about leisure activities they do with friends and why.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure time, Activities, Spend time, Friends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students the questions: 'In your opinion, what activities can we do with our friends in our leisure time?' and 'Why should we spend time with our friends?'.
Students Discuss the questions in pairs and share their answers with the class.
Stage 2: Listening Comprehension - Multiple Choice (10 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice listening comprehension by choosing the correct answers from an interview.
  • Content: Students will listen to an interview with Mark about his leisure activities and choose the correct answers.
  • Target Language/Details: Free time, Interview
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio of the interview and provide students with multiple-choice questions.
Students Listen to the interview and select the correct answer for each question.
Teacher Review the answers with the class and discuss the correct options.
Stage 3: Listening Comprehension - Fill in the Blanks (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To enhance listening skills by filling in blanks with specific information from the interview.
  • Content: Students will listen to the interview again and fill in each blank in the table with no more than TWO words.
  • Target Language/Details: No more than TWO words
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Play the audio of the interview again and provide students with a table to fill in.
Students Listen to the interview and fill in the blanks with the correct information.
Teacher Check the answers with the class and provide feedback.
Stage 4: Pair Work - Ask and Answer Questions (15 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice speaking skills by discussing personal leisure activities with friends.
  • Content: Students will work in pairs to ask and answer questions about their leisure activities.
  • Target Language/Details: Free time, Activities, Friends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Provide students with questions to discuss in pairs: 'When do you usually have free time?', 'What do you usually do with your friends in your free time?', 'Why do you do these activities?'.
Students Take turns asking and answering the questions with their partner.
Teacher Monitor the pairs and provide assistance as needed.
Stage 5: Writing an Email (20 minutes)
  • Aim: To practice writing skills by composing an email about leisure activities with friends.
  • Content: Students will write an email to a penfriend about what they usually do with their friends in their free time.
  • Target Language/Details: Email, Leisure activities, Friends
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to use their answers from Stage 4 to write an email (80-100 words) to a penfriend, starting and ending the email as instructed.
Students Write the email individually, describing their leisure activities with friends.
Teacher Collect the emails and provide feedback on their writing.
Wrap-up (5 minutes)
  • Aim: To review the key points and language from the lesson.
  • Content: The teacher will review the key points from the lesson and ask students to reflect on what they learned.
  • Target Language/Details: Leisure time, Free time, Activities
Role Activities (Step-by-step)
Teacher Ask students to share one new thing they learned about leisure activities or writing emails.
Students Reflect on the lesson and share their thoughts with the class.
